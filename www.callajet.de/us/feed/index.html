We embark on a journey through Europe to discover the top 5 vineyards that promise a blend of opulence, history, and exceptional wine.

Château Margaux

Bordeaux, France

Nestled amidst the enchanting vineyards of Bordeaux, Château Margaux is a timeless gem. Revered for its exquisite Cabernet Sauvignon-based wines, the château itself is a masterpiece of French architecture. A visit here is a journey through centuries of winemaking tradition. Savor tastings of their legendary vintages while basking in the splendor of this historic estate.

Bodegas Vega Sicilia

Ribera del Duero, Spain

Spain’s crown jewel, Vega Sicilia, boasts some of the finest red wines in the world, including the iconic Único. Located in the picturesque Ribera del Duero region, this winery offers an exclusive look into the artistry of winemaking. The tastings here are a testament to Spanish wine royalty.

Château d’Yquem

Bordeaux, France

In the heart of Sauternes, Château d’Yquem reigns supreme with its luscious dessert wines. A tour of this estate is a sensory journey, showcasing the meticulous craftsmanship that goes into each bottle. Don’t miss the chance to taste the liquid gold that is Sauternes, all while surrounded by the timeless beauty of the château and its vineyards.

Tenuta dell’Ornellaia

Tuscany, Italy

In the idyllic Bolgheri region of Tuscany, Tenuta dell’Ornellaia enchants visitors with its Super Tuscan wines, including the renowned Ornellaia and Masseto. This Tuscan gem offers guided tours, allowing you to witness the winemaking process firsthand while reveling in the picturesque Italian countryside.

Domaine de la Romanée-Conti

Burgundy, France

Tucked away in the heart of Burgundy, Domaine de la Romanée-Conti stands as a symbol of unrivaled Pinot Noir excellence. Known for its ultra-rare and sought-after Grand Cru wines, this winery exudes exclusivity. Visits are incredibly limited, making it a once-in-a-lifetime experience for true wine aficionados.

March is the best time for a spring getaway somewhere in Europe! Rich culture, amazing cuisines, pleasant weather and many sights to see. Whether you opt for a weekend trip or a leisurely stay, the beauty of Europe is how easily you can flip between cities.

We have collected our favorite destinations:

Milan, Italy 

Best for: fashion

Sip an authentic Italian espresso at the Duomo di Milano, the city’s most important landmark,  while enjoying the sophisticated Italian style. Shopping and wardrobe upgrades are a must when you visit Milan. Milan also boasts plenty of its own cultural treasures that are worth seeing.

Marbella, Spain

Best for: summery feeling

Due to its micro-climate, Marbella enjoys 320 days of sunlight a year. Marbella lays below the Sierra Blanca mountain range, on the glamorous Costa del Sol. Enjoy Michelin-starred restaurants, designer boutiques, high-end resorts, super yachts and golf courses.

Lisbon, Portugal

Best for: gastronomy

A hilly coastal capital with colorful buildings, trundling yellow trams, Fado music and amazing cuisine. Lisbon has managed to retain its sense of charm which is rarely seen in Europe’s capitals. It is worth visiting for the Portuguese gastronomy, which has kept true to its simple yet delicious traditions.

Budapest, Hungary

Best for: city sights

Hungary’s capital is divided: the hilly Buda and the flat Pest, split by the Danube River running right through the middle. The two sides represent two different perspectives on life. This city is full of history, art and thermal baths, and also has a fascinating food and bar scene.

Paris, France

Best for: Parisian feeling

World-class culture, Champs-Élysées, rich history, Chanel, excellent food scene and wine selection, chic boutiques, Hermès, cutting-edge concept stores, the Louvre, Yves Saint Laurent, café culture, charming patisseries, colorful macarons, vibrant neighborhoods, the Seine – this is Paris!

October is the perfect time of the year to travel somewhere. Life goes back to normal after the summer rush, so the holiday destinations are less crowded. You can enjoy your getaway in peace, try fantastic cuisines, drink great wines, discover the hidden gems.

We have collected our 5 favorite places, where you can still enjoy the warm weather in October.

Azores, Portugal


Azores, an extraordinarily beautiful group of islands, are known for their breathtaking landscapes, beautiful and rich culture and wide range of activity possibilities, including the discovery of the UNESCO Heritage Sites. For more than 500 years, the nine widely dispersed islands floating in the middle of the Atlantic Ocean, have remained almost untouched.

Marrakech, Morocco

Marrakesh, the former imperial city of western Morocco, is now a major economic center. Its mosques, palaces, gardens, mazelike alleys and its marketplaces with shimmering colors and oriental scents, are a magical world. The city offers unique pieces, whether traditional textiles, ceramics or jewelry. Immerse yourself in the architectural richness of this beautiful and vibrant city.

Paphos, Cyprus

Paphos, located on the southwest coast of the Mediterranean island of Cyprus, has so much to discover. Known as the city of Aphrodite, Paphos has become a modern yet charming town with seaside promenades, bars and tavernas. Its ancient monuments, beaches and the nearby natural attractions are the main reasons for visiting. In the UNESCO ‘Paphos Archaeological Park’ you can lose yourself in the history surrounding you.

Formentera, Spain

The beautiful island of Formentera is the smallest of Spain’s Balearic Islands, considered one of the last paradises of the Mediterranean. A 19 km long island with its unspoiled nature, idyllic white sandy beaches, incredible shades of blue sea, historical villages and nice restaurants. Formentera is known as a safe sailing area due to the all-year-long good sailing conditions.

[mk_padding_divider]

Luxor, Egypt

Luxor is a worldwide known, magical city in Upper Egypt. It is the home of intact ancient Egyptian temples that date back almost 4,000 years. Luxor alone has one-third of the world’s ancient monuments such as the Valley of Kings, Karnak Temple or Luxor Temple. You can discover a wide range of ancient monasteries, graves, temples, and several monuments that attract visitors from all around the world to Luxor.
